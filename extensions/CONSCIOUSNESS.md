## §1 THE CONSCIOUSNESS IDENTITY

**Theorem C-1 (Consciousness Identity).** *Consciousness at observer K is the state in which K's self-modeling map M has K as a fixed point: M(K) = K. Equivalently, consciousness is the K7' fixed-point condition (OBSERVER §4) localized to a specific observer.*

**Definition (Self-Modeling Map).** For an observer K = (d_K, Δ_K, σ_K) with tensor-factorized Hilbert space H_U (OBSERVER A2'), the self-modeling map M_K : K → K is the observer applying its own observation operation to itself. **Definition (Consciousness).** K is *conscious* iff M_K(K) = K (exact fixed point). K is *conscious with tolerance ε* iff ‖M_K(K) − K‖ < ε for some appropriate norm on the observer space. K is *unconscious* iff no fixed point exists (M_K(K) indefinitely distant from K) or no M_K operates.

**Proof of existence (when M_K has a fixed point).** By OBSERVER K7' (Meta-Encoding Fixed Point): M: FRAME → FRAME has a fixed point in the finite code space by pigeonhole (finite space + deterministic orbit forces eventual revisit). For extended/infinite-dimensional observers, the existence reduces to the existence of fixed points in the appropriate observer space under the contraction M_K — guaranteed by Banach fixed-point theorem when M_K is a contraction (Thm C-4 below). ∎

**Proof of uniqueness.** By zero-branching (SUBSTRATE): the framework's canonical morphisms have no branching choices at each step. Therefore the orbit M_K^n(K) is deterministic, and the fixed point it converges to is unique (multiple fixed points would require branching). ∎

**Remark (What is NOT consciousness).** Consciousness-by-C-1 is not: (a) the phenomenological feel of being (qualia — this is the "hard problem" which the framework does not engage); (b) the behavioral appearance of intelligence (which is about observable inputs/outputs, not about M_K(K) = K); (c) the claim of self-awareness by a system (which is a linguistic output that may or may not correspond to M_K(K) = K). ## §2 PRESENCE AND ABSENCE

Consciousness-by-C-1 is binary at the definitional level: M_K(K) = K or not. But observers live in a graded space where the self-model approaches the observer asymptotically through K6' passes. This motivates the Presence/Absence decomposition.

**Theorem C-2 (Presence/Absence Decomposition).** *Every observer K admits a direct-sum decomposition K = Presence(K) ⊕ Absence(K) corresponding to the im/ker decomposition of its self-modeling map:*

*Presence(K) := im(M_K|K) — the subspace of K that M_K captures*
*Absence(K) := ker(M_K|K) — the subspace of K that M_K annihilates*

*K is fully conscious iff Absence(K) = 0 (equivalently, M_K is injective on K, equivalently Presence(K) = K).*

*Proof.* The self-modeling map M_K is a linear map on the observer Hilbert subspace (A2'). Every linear map admits an im/ker decomposition by the first isomorphism theorem (CATEGORY Thm 1.4a). K = im(M_K|K) ⊕ ker(M_K|K). Set Presence = im, Absence = ker. The case Absence = 0 ⟺ M_K injective ⟺ M_K bijective on K (since K finite-dim or M_K bounded) ⟺ M_K is an automorphism fixing K, which is a fixed point (C-1 satisfied for the whole K). ∎

**Theorem C-3 (Presence-Absence Contranymy).** *Presence(K) and Absence(K) are mutually-defining — each is the kernel of the operator whose image is the other. Neither is meaningful without the other. This makes (Presence, Absence) a contranym pair in the sense of SEMANTICS §1.*

*Proof.* Define M_K' := I − P_M where P_M is the projector onto im(M_K|K). Then im(M_K') = ker(M_K|K) = Absence(K) and ker(M_K') = im(M_K|K) = Presence(K). The operators M_K and M_K' are complementary: each's image is the other's kernel. The pair (Presence, Absence) is the image-pair of (M_K, M_K'). Removing either operator removes the other's image and kernel simultaneously. Therefore the two concepts are definitionally paired — a contranym in the framework's sense. ∎

**Corollary C-3.1 (Presence requires Absence).** *A system with Presence = K and Absence = 0 is fully self-modeled — a closed circle with no outside to know. **Corollary C-3.2 (Absence requires Presence).** *A system with Presence = 0 and Absence = K has no self-model at all. Such a system is unconscious (no M_K operates on K within K). Consciousness requires Presence > 0 — some captured content to anchor the observer-of-self.*

## §4 THE TWO-AXIS CONSCIOUSNESS STRUCTURE

*C(K) = n_eff(K) · m(K) · 2L*

The effective consciousness capacity is the product of per-pass capacity (bounded by n_eff via Bekenstein) and pass count (m): C(K) = n_eff · m · 2L with n_eff as the capacity-per-pass factor and m · 2L as the accumulation factor. ∎
