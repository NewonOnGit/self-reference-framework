## §3 OBSERVATION BASIS DECOMPOSITION

The observation basis {O⁺, O⁻, |O⁻⟩⟨O⁺|, |O⁺⟩⟨O⁻|} (ALGEBRA §8) gives computation theory native algebraic machinery. Every computation decomposes into intra-channel (how f acts within each observation channel) and cross-channel (how f transfers between channels) components.

**Theorem C.15 (Observation Basis Decomposition).** *Every M ∈ M₂(ℝ) decomposes uniquely as:*

M = a⁺ O⁺ + a⁻ O⁻ + c₊ |O⁻⟩⟨O⁺| + c₋ |O⁺⟩⟨O⁻|

*where a⁺ = consensus-channel weight, a⁻ = selection-channel weight, c₊ c₋ = cross-channel weights.*

*Proof.* {O⁺, O⁻, |O⁻⟩⟨O⁺|, |O⁺⟩⟨O⁻|} has rank 4 in M₂(ℝ), hence spans and the decomposition is unique. ∎

**Three key invariants through the observation basis:**
- tr(M) = a⁺ + a⁻ (total channel weight)
- det(M) = a⁺a⁻ − c₊c₋ (intra-channel product minus cross-channel product)
- disc(M) = (a⁺−a⁻)² + 4c₊c₋ (channel asymmetry plus cross-channel coupling)

Framework generator decompositions:

| Generator | a⁺ | a⁻ | c₊ | c₋ | Cross symmetry |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|-----|---------------|
| R | 1/2 | 1/2 | √5/2 | √5/2 | SYMMETRIC |
| N | 0 | 0 | 1 | −1 | ANTISYMMETRIC |
| I | 1 | 1 | 0 | 0 | ZERO |

**The orbit type trichotomy IS the cross-channel symmetry classification:**

- **Type I (P1, det < 0):** SYMMETRIC cross-channel coupling (c₊c₋ > 0, same sign). Production transfers between observation channels in the same direction. Cross-channel product exceeds intra-channel product.
- **Type III (P3, det > 0, Δ < 0):** ANTISYMMETRIC cross-channel coupling (c₊c₋ < 0, opposite sign). Observation transfers between channels in opposite directions. The channels anti-correlate.
- **Type II (P2, det > 0, Δ > 0):** MIXED or WEAK cross-channel. Intra-channel dominates.

R has symmetric cross-channel coupling — production REQUIRES crossing between observation channels. N has antisymmetric coupling — observation IS the anti-correlated oscillation between channels. The cross-channel symmetry IS the P1/P3 structural distinction made algebraic.

**Theorem C.16 (Cross-Channel Content).** *CC(M) = |c₊c₋|/(a⁺a⁻ + |c₊c₋|) measures the fraction of computational content in cross-channel transfer. For matrices with a⁺ = a⁻ (equal channel weights): CC = disc(M)/(disc(M) + tr(M)²).*

*Values: CC(R) = disc(R)/(disc(R)+1) = 5/6 — the productive generator devotes 5/6 of its content to cross-channel transfer. CC(N) = 1 — pure cross-channel. CC(I) = 0 — pure intra-channel.* ∎

**Theorem C.17½ (Type III Channel Balance).** *All Type III computations exp(θN) have ρ = a⁻/(a⁺+a⁻) = 1/2 for all θ. The rotation group SO(2) lives entirely on the ρ = 1/2 line in Phase-Dist space.*

*Proof.* exp(θN) = cos(θ)I + sin(θ)N. I has a⁺ = a⁻ = 1, N has a⁺ = a⁻ = 0. So exp(θN) has a⁺ = a⁻ = cos(θ). Channel balance: ρ = cos(θ)/(2cos(θ)) = 1/2 for all θ with cos(θ) ≠ 0. ∎

The Ch-Maj gap in SHA-256 (SHA256 §2) is the observation basis decomposition made concrete: ‖corr(Ch)‖²/‖corr(Maj)‖² = 2/3 = Q_Koide (the power ratio IS the Koide ratio), and HW([Maj,Ch])/64 = 3/8 = sin²θ_W (the disagreement rate IS the Casimir invariant). The discriminant disc(R) = 4c² = 5 IS the squared cross-channel content of R: SHA-256 computes at the full noncommutative norm of the framework.
